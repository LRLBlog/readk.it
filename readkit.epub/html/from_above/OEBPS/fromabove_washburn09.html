Glaciers are excellent natural indicators of regional climate change. On these large valley glaciers there exist elevation contours with annual ice accumulation above and ice loss below. Long term changes in winter precipitation or summer temperatures cause these equilibrium lines to move uphill or downhill. —Bo Svoma, Climatologist & Physical Geographer
