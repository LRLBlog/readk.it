Marilyn Bridges, United States b. 1948. Mount Saint Helens Volcano after the Eruption, 1981. Gelatin silver print. Gift of Steven Soter 2011.51.2. © Marilyn Bridges 1981

Ruins and monuments are documents of power on the landscape. In this haunting photograph, a volcano that ruined cities and human lives is a monument to nature’s might. Marilyn Bridges captures the harrowing violence of nature’s battleground and the poetry of her force: she can spew out fire, turn creatures to ash and cover the land in tree shrapnel. —Casandra Hernandez, Education & Programs Manager, ASU Deer Valley Rock Art Center

A beautiful, artistic image fills the frame. In reality, it is the mass demolition of a mammoth forest—300-foot trees turned to match sticks by volcanic force. This image evokes personal reaction. As a cub TV reporter in the Northwest, I covered the 1980 Mt. St. Helens eruption. I saw this same incredible scene. —Craig Allen, Journalism Professor
