COMMENTATOR

Casandra Hernandez

Casandra Hernandez is Education and Programs Manager at the ASU Deer Valley Rock Art Center, a museum, Native American rock art site, and nature preserve in Phoenix. A native of Hermosillo, Sonora, she holds a Master’s degree in Anthropology and Museum Studies from Arizona State University. Her expertise is in museum education, arts and culture programming, exhibit curation, Sonoran Desert anthropology and ecology, and cultural representation in museums. Casandra is currently an invited collaborator with ASU Performance in the Borderlands, co-producing events and cultural performances such as Breaking Boundaries: Art, Innovation and Social Engagement (Phoenix Center for the Arts, Sept. 25, 2012), Beyond Miss Representation (A.E. England, Feb.12, 2013) and Edible Stories: From Desert Foods to Food Deserts (Scottsdale Museum of Contemporary Art, coming June 13, 2013). Casandra has served on the board of directors of the Central Arizona Museum Association and the Arizona Latino Arts and Cultural Center. Recent exhibitions include: Latino Arizona: 100 Years at the Arizona Latino Arts and Cultural Center (with Jose Benavides, Marco Albarrán and Jose Andres Giron), Leaving Marks: The Rock Art and Archaeology of Deer Valley (with Kimberly Arth) and The Green Room: The People, Plants and Animals of Hedgpeth Hills at the Deer Valley Rock Art Center. In 2009, Casandra organized with April Bojorquez and Peter Welsh the national symposium Exhibiting Ourselves: Representing Cultures in Museums and Cultural Centers at the Arizona Historical Society Museum at Papago Park.
