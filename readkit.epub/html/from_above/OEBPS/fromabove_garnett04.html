William Garnett, United States, 1916–2006. Untitled, 1965. Gelatin silver print. Gift of Ansel and Virginia Adams 76.317.2. © William A. Garnett Estate. Not included in final exhibition.

This is not only a powerful abstract image but the fractal patterns really jump out at me. There is not only the strong fractal patterns at the bottom, at what I take to be a river mouth, but there are much more delicate patterns in upper left where you’re looking at what I think are salt deposits at the side of a flow, where the water flowed out and evaporated. There must be something fundamental about repeating fractal patterns that resonates with human beings because we’re just as thrilled by looking at a really well-developed tree branching out as we are at looking at a river from above. —Stephen Strom, Astronomer
