As a cartographer, I respect the mystery of aerial photography. Making a map, I use lines, shapes, colors and text to tell a particular story about a place. In aerial photography, the story is told by the sun’s energy reflecting off the earth’s surface. The results can be intriguing, nuanced, ambiguous, beautiful or all of these things. —Barbara Trapido-Lurie, Cartographer
