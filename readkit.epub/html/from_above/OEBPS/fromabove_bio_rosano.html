COMMENTATOR

Teresa Rosano

Ibarra Rosano Design Architects, Principal Architect Teresa Rosano (AIA LEED AP) is a native Tucsonan, and a graduate of and adjunct lecturer at the University of Arizona College of Architecture. She is co-founder and principal architect of IBARRA ROSANO DESIGN ARCHITECTS, Inc., which has earned national recognition as one of Arizona's top design firms for its modern desert architecture. The firm’s work has been published internationally and has received over fifty regional, national and international design awards.

In 2002 Architecture Magazine recognized Ibarra Rosano in its issue on the "Arizona School" featuring the state’s design vanguards. Their work has twice been exhibited in the Scottsdale Museum of Contemporary Art where they were also commissioned for an exhibit installation. They were nominated for a Cooper Hewitt Design Award in 2006. In 2008 Residential Architect Magazine awarded Ibarra Rosano with their "Rising Star National Leadership Award” and were included among 50 international firms in their "RA50: Short List of Architects We Love". They were recently featured on HGTV's "Extreme Homes".

Teresa is well regarded as a leading female architect practitioner who has been invited to lecture internationally and serve on numerous national design juries and panel discussions on architectural practice and its future.
